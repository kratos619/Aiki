Build a pickup-only marketplace that lets restaurants sell unsold food near closing time as $5 mystery bags.
The platform keeps 25% of each sale. The founder plans to launch across one mid-sized city with a $120,000
twelve-month budget. Twelve restaurants have signed non-binding letters of interest, but no restaurant has run a
paid pilot and no consumer-demand data exists. The plan assumes ten bags sold per restaurant per day.

Restaurants would declare available bags manually. Consumers would pay in the app and collect during a thirty-minute
window. The company would handle refunds and marketplace support but would not store, inspect, or deliver food. Food
allergen disclosure, safe-holding responsibility, refund rates, payment fees, restaurant churn, and neighborhood
density have not been validated.

Decide whether to build and launch citywide, run a concierge pilot in one dense neighborhood, change the model, or
stop. Address both sides of the marketplace, unit economics, food-safety responsibility, operational friction,
retention, and measurable kill criteria.
