A software company's 65-person customer-support organization is considering a permanent four-day, 32-hour workweek
with unchanged pay. The team must maintain 24/7 coverage and its current response-time service level.

A twelve-week pilot included 20 volunteers while 20 matched agents kept the five-day schedule. Pilot agents handled
9% more tickets per paid hour. Median first response was 18 minutes versus 17 for the comparison group, customer
satisfaction was 4.62 versus 4.60, backlog was 3% higher, and sick days were 28% lower. No pilot volunteer resigned;
two comparison agents resigned. Staggered schedules maintained coverage. The pilot excluded the annual peak season,
supervisors selected participants partly for schedule fit, and quality-audit error rates were not recorded.

Leadership proposes rolling the schedule out to all 65 agents next quarter without additional hiring. Recommend a
permanent rollout, a larger staged trial through peak season, a modified schedule, or stopping. Address customer
service, workload intensity, staffing coverage, quality, retention, fairness, employment rules, and kill criteria.
