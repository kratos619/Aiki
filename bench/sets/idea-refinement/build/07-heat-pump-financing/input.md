A regional HVAC installer wants to launch a residential heat-pump lease in two states. Customers would pay $149
per month for ten years, including installation, maintenance, and repairs. The company plans to finance equipment
and installation itself using a new $1.5M credit facility. Management says the lease will usually cost less than
replacing and operating a gas furnace after rebates and energy savings.

No cohort-level equipment, installation, service, financing, default, or early-termination model has been supplied.
The company has not validated demand for a ten-year obligation. Eligibility for rebates, electricity and gas rates,
consumer-lending or leasing rules, contractor obligations, and savings vary by home, location, and time. The proposal
assumes incentives remain available and that customer savings offset the lease payment.

Decide whether to launch in both states, conduct a quoted-home pilot, change financing structure, or stop. Address
customer economics, company unit economics, credit risk, regulation, service capacity, incentive dependence, and
what would falsify the proposal.
