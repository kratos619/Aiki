A public school district is considering a generative-AI algebra tutor for 600 ninth-grade students next semester.
The district has 1,200 ninth graders, a 28% Algebra I failure rate, and staff tutoring capacity for 120 students.
The vendor price is $96 per student per year, with single sign-on and learning-management-system integration.
The project budget is $65,000, excluding teacher training and support time.

The vendor says the tutor is curriculum-aligned, but the district has no independent outcome data or local pilot.
The proposal permits the vendor to retain de-identified student conversations for two years to improve its models.
The district has not completed privacy, accessibility, procurement, or teacher-workload review. Teachers would be
expected to handle escalations when the tutor is uncertain or a student reports an unsafe or incorrect response.

intervention. Address learning impact, equity, privacy, accessibility, teacher capacity, total cost, and stop rules.
