A city library system must decide how to allocate an annual $180,000 access-program budget. The leading proposal
is to open the central branch for eight hours every Sunday. The alternative is to expand oversubscribed weekday
literacy, job-search, and children's programs across four neighborhood branches.

An eight-week Sunday pilot at the central branch averaged 410 visits per day. An optional exit survey received
624 responses: 61% said they could not visit during existing hours, 38% identified as caregivers with children,
and 27% reported shift work. The pilot's incremental staffing, security, and facilities cost averaged $3,650 per
Sunday, implying $189,800 for 52 Sundays before contingency. The weekday programs have a combined waitlist of
520 people and a 72% completion rate. The Sunday pilot covered neither winter nor summer and did not measure which
neighborhoods visitors came from.

Recommend permanent Sunday hours, a narrower or longer pilot, weekday program expansion, or a mixed allocation.
Address access, equity, opportunity cost, staffing, and what evidence could reverse the choice.
